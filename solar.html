Rooftops, Shade And Solar Heat

Could island roofs, shade structures, ferry-gateway car parks and solar thermal tests become the first clean energy layer?

First power station

Could every useful roof become part of the island's energy brain?

Rooftop solar is the least weird part of the stack. The deeper question is how homes, businesses, clubs and public roofs can act together without taking control away from owners.

  • Which roofs already produce power, and which are still waiting?
  • Which loads matter most: fridges, pumps, workshops, e-bikes, ferries, lights, kiosks or emergency phones?
  • Could hot water and thermal storage soak up midday solar before money is spent on complex hardware?

Solar heat

Could concentration be a workshop question before it is a power-station claim?

Concentrated solar thermal can make high-grade heat and store it. On an island, the public first question may be smaller: can clean heat dry, cook, sterilise, warm water, support a workshop or charge a sand store?
