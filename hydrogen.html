Water-energy loop

Could green hydrogen become a loop people can inspect?

Green hydrogen starts with renewable electricity and water electrolysis. On a sand island, the richer question is wider: where does the water come from, could desalination be powered cleanly, what does the brine become, how are oxygen and heat used, and could the value make Moreton Bay repair work more resourced and visible over time?

  1. Start with water. Could a public desal bench show litres, energy, membranes, maintenance, brine concentration and water quality in plain English?

  2. Split the loop open. Could electrolysis make hydrogen, oxygen, heat, data and learning visible before a larger fuel pathway takes shape?

  3. Let the bay benefit. Could mineral recovery, sensors, seagrass knowledge, catchment repair and public report-card work sit in the same conversation?

A hydrogen idea gets more interesting when it becomes a water, minerals, oxygen, transport, data and Moreton Bay repair question.
