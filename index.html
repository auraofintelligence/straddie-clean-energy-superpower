The core move

Start with useful energy, not energy theatre.

A clean-energy superpower can mean more than a giant industrial build. It could mean roofs that work harder, public buildings that store heat, emergency nodes that stay alive, grants that fund real tests, and local people who can understand the system.

Why a repo?

Because the public map can be inspectable.

This site is not an energy plan, engineering design or endorsement claim. It is a public workbench for turning big clean-energy imagination into clear questions, source trails, builder forms and next-small-step notes.

  1. Ask the plain question. What problem does this option solve for residents, Country, business, ferry flow, emergency resilience or community wealth?

  2. Find the smallest harmless test. Could a tabletop model, data check, roof audit, workshop bench or digital twin answer the first doubt?

  3. Name the open checks. Who could help inspect culture, ecology, pressure, response systems, water, finance, data and public claims before momentum builds?

If the idea is strong, it can handle plain English, public sources and people saying no.
