Pumped-hydro lessons

Could Snowy-scale thinking help a sand island ask better questions?

Snowy 2.0, Wivenhoe, Kidston, Borumba and the ANU pumped-hydro atlas prove that water-height storage is a serious human tool. Straddie is a different kind of place: ocean, bay, sand hills, dune ridges, perched lakes, wetlands, aquifers, sands and mineral sands. The informal question starts by learning the logic of height, time, tunnels, tanks, reversible flow, modelling and grid support, then asking what the island's own map reveals.

  1. Study real pumped hydro with respect. What do Snowy, Wivenhoe, Kidston and Borumba teach about scale, duration, reservoirs, tunnels, pumps, reversible generation and public trust?

  2. Map Straddie as Straddie. What do ocean edge, bay edge, dune height, sandmass infiltration, aquifers, perched lakes, wetlands and mineral sands suggest, invite or reshape?

  3. Let models become shared capability. Could tank pairs, digital twins, school rigs and maker-space demos help residents see the physics before any larger informal idea grows legs?

A happy clean-energy island can power more than lights: learning, compute, transport, food, repair, culture and local confidence.
